## 1. Nordic Model (Nordic Europe)

### 1.1 Overview
- Nordic welfare state model: Denmark, Norway, Sweden, Finland, Iceland
- Universalism, high taxation, strong public sector
- Social democratic tradition

### 1.2 Concrete Benefit Amounts (Adults, Unemployed, 2024–2025)

*Sources: official gov/NAV/Kela/A-kassa; amounts in USD (approx. 1 EUR ≈ 1.08 USD, 1 DKK ≈ 0.14 USD, 1 SEK ≈ 0.095 USD, 1 NOK ≈ 0.09 USD).*

| Country | Program | Who Qualifies | Amount (approx.) | Notes |
|---------|---------|--------------|------------------|-------|
| **Denmark** | Dagpenge (unemployment insurance) | 12+ months in A-kasse, income threshold met | **~$3,090/mo** (DKK 22,041) max (2026) | 90% of prior salary cap; loyalty supplement up to ~$3,670 first 3 months |
| **Denmark** | Kontanthjælp (social assistance) | No insurance, cannot support self | **~$950–1,750/mo** (DKK 6,789–12,498) | Min 6,789; basic 7,205; increased 12,498 if 9yr residence + 2.5yr work; ~38% tax above 4,300 |
| **Sweden** | A-kassa (unemployment insurance) | 12mo membership, 120k SEK earned | **Up to ~$2,580/mo** (27,200 SEK) max (2025) | 80% of income (cap 34k); 70% days 101–200; 65% days 201–300 |
| **Sweden** | Försörjningsstöd (social assistance) | No insurance, means-tested | **~$370 base + rent** (3,910 SEK + housing) | Riksnorm covers food, clothes, etc.; rent added by municipality |
| **Norway** | Dagpenger (unemployment benefit) | 1.5G or 3G earned in 12/36 months | **~62.4% of prior income** | Min income 195k NOK (1.5G); no fixed floor; child supplement ~185 NOK/wk |
| **Norway** | Økonomisk sosialhjelp | No income, exhausted other options | **Discretionary** | Municipality assesses needs; no statutory minimum |
| **Finland** | Peruspäiväraha (basic allowance) | Some work history, jobseeker | **~$865/mo** (€37.21/day) | 5 days/week; work requirement |
| **Finland** | Earnings-related (työttömyyskorvaus) | 6mo membership, income history | **~$1,670–2,220/mo** (examples) | Depends on prior salary; basic €37.21/day + earnings component |

**Bottom line (floor for adult with no job, no insurance):** Denmark kontanthjælp ~$950/mo; Sweden försörjningsstöd ~$370 + rent; Norway discretionary; Finland requires work history for basic allowance (~$865/mo).

### 1.3 Safety Net Components
- **Unemployment insurance**: Generosity, duration, eligibility
- **Healthcare**: Universal access, funding, coverage
- **Pensions**: Public vs. private, replacement rates
- **Housing**: Social housing, rent control, homelessness
- **Childcare & family support**: Parental leave, childcare subsidies
- **Education**: Free higher education, student support
- **Minimum income / social assistance**: Floor for those outside employment

### 1.4 Institutional Features
- Tripartite bargaining (unions, employers, state)
- Active labor market policies
- Decentralization vs. centralization

## 2. Continental Europe (EU / Non-Nordic)

### 2.1 Overview
- Bismarckian vs. Beveridge models
- Key countries: Germany, France, Netherlands, Belgium, Austria
- EU-level coordination and directives

### 2.2 Concrete Benefit Amounts (Adults, Unemployed, 2024–2025)

*Amounts in USD (approx. 1 EUR ≈ 1.08 USD).*

| Country | Program | Who Qualifies | Amount (approx.) | Notes |
|---------|---------|--------------|------------------|-------|
| **Germany** | Arbeitslosengeld I (ALG I) | 12mo contributions in past 30mo | **60–67% of prior net** (67% with children) | Max ~$2,850–3,700/mo; duration up to 24mo by age |
| **Germany** | Bürgergeld (basic security) | No ALG I, job-seeking, assets <€40k | **~$610/mo** (€563) + housing | Regelsatz covers living; rent (KdU) paid separately |
| **France** | Chômage (ARE) | 6mo work in past 24mo, involuntary job loss | **Income-based** (replacement rate) | No fixed min/max; duration up to 24–36mo; high earners can receive €6k+ |
| **France** | RSA (minimum income) | Low/no income, registered with France Travail | **~$700/mo** (€646.52) max (2025) | Differential allowance; supplements to reach this; 15h/wk activity required |
| **Netherlands** | WW (unemployment) | 26+ weeks work in past 36 weeks | **75% first 2mo, 70% after** of prior wage | Max ~$6,800/mo (€6,478); daily wage cap €297.82 |
| **Netherlands** | Bijstand (social assistance) | No other benefits, low assets (<€7,605) | **~$1,440/mo** (€1,331) single | 100% standard; 110% = ~$1,580; housing often separate |
| **Belgium** | Unemployment (min) | 12mo work in past 36mo | **~$1,500/mo** (€1,390) single (2025) | Min amounts; head of household ~$1,850; cohabitant ~$1,335 |
| **Belgium** | RIS (Revenu d'intégration) | No job, no unemployment rights | **~$1,390–1,420/mo** (€1,288–1,314) single | Indexed; cohabitant without dependents ~$930 |
| **Austria** | Arbeitslosengeld | 6mo work in past 12mo | **~€41/day** (~$950/mo) avg | Income-based; ~62% of prior net; duration 20–52 weeks |
| **Austria** | Mindestsicherung / Sozialhilfe | No income, exhausted other options | **~$1,250/mo** (€1,156) single | Varies by Bundesland; +30% for housing possible (~$1,620) |

**Bottom line (floor for adult with no job, no insurance):** Germany Bürgergeld ~$610 + rent; France RSA ~$700; Netherlands bijstand ~$1,440; Belgium RIS ~$1,390; Austria Mindestsicherung ~$1,250.

### 2.3 Safety Net Components
- **Unemployment**: Insurance-based (Germany) vs. universal (UK legacy)
- **Healthcare**: Mandatory insurance, statutory vs. private
- **Pensions**: Contribution-based, multi-pillar systems
- **Housing**: Varying approaches; limited social housing in some
- **Family policy**: Maternity/paternity leave, childcare
- **Minimum income**: Guaranteed minimum income schemes (e.g., RSA in France)

### 2.4 Institutional Features
- Social insurance vs. tax-funded
- Role of employers and social partners
- EU social policy and cohesion funds

## 3. United States

### 3.1 Overview
- Liberal welfare regime (Esping-Andersen)
- Federal vs. state responsibility
- Market-oriented, means-tested programs

### 3.2 Concrete Benefit Amounts (Adults, Unemployed, 2024–2025)

*All amounts in USD. State-by-state variation is large; figures are illustrative.*

| Program | Who Qualifies | Amount (approx.) | Notes |
|---------|---------------|------------------|-------|
| **Unemployment Insurance (UI)** | Prior work, involuntary job loss; varies by state | **$960–3,700/mo** (weekly $221–$855) | MA max $855/wk (~$3,700/mo); LA min $221/wk (~$960/mo); most states 26 weeks |
| **SNAP (food stamps)** | Low income, asset limits | **$292/mo** single (48 states) | Food only; no cash; Alaska/Hawaii higher |
| **TANF (cash assistance)** | Primarily families with children | **$204–1,151/mo** (family of 3) | Most states exclude childless able-bodied adults; benefit by state |
| **SSI** | Disabled or 65+; very low assets | **$967/mo** single (2025) | Federal max; not for able-bodied unemployed |
| **General Assistance (GA)** | Childless adults; 26 states + DC | **Variable** | CT $200/mo; most states $200–$500; many states have no GA |

**Bottom line (floor for able-bodied adult with no job, no children):** No federal cash floor. SNAP ~$292/mo (food only). Cash: GA in ~26 states, often $200–$500/mo; many states offer nothing. TANF largely excludes childless adults. Effective floor in many states: **$292 (food) + $0 (cash)**.

### 3.3 Safety Net Components
- **Unemployment**: State-administered UI; limited duration and coverage
- **Healthcare**: Employer-based insurance, Medicare, Medicaid, ACA
- **Pensions**: Social Security, 401(k), defined benefit decline
- **Housing**: Section 8, public housing; homelessness crisis
- **Family support**: No federal paid leave; limited childcare
- **Minimum income**: No federal minimum; TANF, SNAP, EITC as patchwork

### 3.4 Institutional Features
- Fragmentation by state
- Work requirements and conditionality
- Private sector and philanthropy as supplements
